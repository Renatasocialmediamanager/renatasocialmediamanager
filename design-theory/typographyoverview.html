What is Typography?

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing when displayed. It involves the careful selection of typefaces, point sizes, line lengths, line-spacing, and letter-spacing. In digital marketing and social media, typography is a key tool for establishing a brand's "voice" and hierarchy.

10 Essential Typography Terms

1. Typeface
A particular design of type (e.g., Helvetica or Times New Roman). It is the "creative" design of the letters.
2. Font
The specific style or size of a typeface (e.g., Helvetica Bold, 12pt). If a typeface is the "song," the font is the "MP3 file."
3. Serif
The small decorative strokes or "feet" at the ends of character lines (e.g., Georgia).
4. Sans Serif
Typefaces that do not have the small decorative strokes at the ends of characters (e.g., Arial).
5. Kerning
The spacing between individual letter pairs.
6. Tracking
The uniform adjustment of spacing across a whole range of characters.
7. Leading
The vertical distance between lines of text.
8. Hierarchy
Visual arrangement of text that guides the reader’s eye.
9. Baseline
The invisible line upon which most letters "sit."
10. X-Height
The height of lowercase letters, specifically the lowercase "x."
