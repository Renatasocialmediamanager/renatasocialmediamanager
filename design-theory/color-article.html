The Psychology of Palette

By Renata Oliveira

In web design, color is more than just decoration; it is a silent language that tells the user how to feel before they even read a single word. Today, we look at how two brands use color theory to define their identity.

1. Tiffany & Co: Signature Hue

Monochromatic Luxury

Tiffany & Co. uses "Tiffany Blue" to create an immediate sense of luxury and calm. By using vast amounts of white space to let the signature blue pop, they prove that a single hue can define an entire global brand.

2. LEGO: The Triadic Energy

High Saturation Play

LEGO utilizes a Triadic Color Scheme (Red, Yellow, and Blue) to evoke childhood creativity and high energy. These primary colors guide the eye toward action, making it a masterclass in using color for engagement.

The Verdict

Color theory isn't about which colors are "better"—it's about alignment. The palette must always match the brand's unique voice to be effective.
