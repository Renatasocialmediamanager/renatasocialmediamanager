Choosing the Right Voice: A Guide to Font Types

By Renata Oliveira

In social media management, a font isn't just a letter; it's an emotion. Choosing the wrong font type can confuse your audience and dilute your brand message. Here are the four primary categories of fonts and when to use them.

1. Serif Fonts

The Classic Elegance

Description: Serif fonts feature small "feet" or strokes at the ends of the letters. They are associated with tradition, reliability, and high-end luxury.

Best for: Editorial content, law firms, or luxury fashion brands (like Tiffany & Co.).

Download Playfair Display

2. Sans Serif Fonts

Modern & Clean

Description: "Sans" means "without" in French. These fonts lack the small feet, offering a clean, minimalist, and approachable look.

Best for: Tech companies, modern startups, and mobile-first social media content.

Download Montserrat

3. Script Fonts

Personal & Artistic

Description: These fonts mimic human handwriting or calligraphy. They add a personal, feminine, or creative touch to a design.

Best for: Wedding invitations, beauty brands, or "lifestyle" influencer content.

Download Dancing Script

4. Monospaced Fonts

Technical & Precise

Description: In these fonts, every character takes up the exact same amount of horizontal space. This creates a "typewriter" or "coding" aesthetic.

Best for: Displaying code, technical documentation, or creating a retro/tech vibe.
