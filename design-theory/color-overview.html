Color Theory Overview

Digital Strategy & Visual Communication

Color theory is the art and science of color interaction. For a content creator, it is the foundation of building a brand that feels authentic and visually balanced.

1. Hue
The pure spectrum color; the name of the color.
2. Saturation
The intensity or vividness of a color.
3. Value
The relative lightness or darkness of a color.
4. Tint
A color produced by adding white to a pure hue.
5. Shade
A color produced by adding black to a pure hue.
6. Tone
A color produced by adding gray to a pure hue.
7. Primary Colors
Red, Yellow, and Blue.
8. Secondary Colors
Green, Orange, and Purple.
9. Tertiary Colors
Mixed from primary and secondary colors (e.g., Blue-Green).
10. Color Wheel
A circular diagram showing the relationship between colors.
11. Complementary
Colors opposite each other on the wheel.
12. Analogous
Colors sitting next to each other on the wheel.
13. Monochromatic
Variations of a single hue.
14. Triadic
Three colors equally spaced on the wheel.
15. Split-Complementary
A base color plus the two colors adjacent to its complement.
16. Warm Colors
Colors like Red and Orange that evoke energy.
17. Cool Colors
Colors like Blue and Green that evoke calm.
18. Neutral Colors
White, Black, Gray, and Brown.
19. RGB
Digital color model (Red, Green, Blue).
20. CMYK
Print color model (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Black).
